DETAILS

About:-

  • The period of first generation:1946-1959.
  • The first generation laptop was ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Calculator).
  • Vacuum tube based.
  • Magnetic drums used for memory.
  • Too large in size.
  • Induce a large amount of heat due to the vacuum tubes.
  • They were unreliable

About:-

  • The period of second generation:1956-1963.
  • Transistor based.
  • Reduced size.
  • Solution to heat generation.
  • Invention and use of high level languages such as Fortran and Cobol.
  • Improvement of speed and reliability.
  • It still required frequent maintenance.

About:-

  • The period of third generation was 1964-1971.
  • Integrated Circuit (IC) chips were used in computers.
  • In an IC chip, multiple transistors are placed on a silicon chip.
  • The computation time was in nanoseconds.
  • Smaller in size as compared to second generation.
  • Standardisation of COBOL (COBOL 68) was another major development during this period.

About:-

  • The period of fourth generation was 1976-1999.
  • The computing time is in picoseconds.
  • The fourth generation computers were made using very large scale integration technology.
  • This generation of computers supported Graphical User Interface(GUI)
  • All types of High level languages can be used in this type of computers

About:-

  • The period of fifth generation:2000-onwards.
  • In the fifth generation, VLSI technology became ULSI (Ultra Large Scale Integration) technology.
  • Artificial Intelligence is being built into the computer.
  • These computers will be able to think and take decisions.
  • This generation is based on parallel processing hardware and AI (Artificial Intelligence) software.
